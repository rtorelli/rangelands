Ag Climate Tools

Mission

The western United States region's research community has made advances in characterizing the impacts of climate change on cattle production. However, there is a significant gap in translating this data into specific management implications that are relevant and useful for risk analysis and decision making. Our vision is to create a tool that will help users view and analyze data.

Vision

This website is a means to explore and understand changing cattle production pressures in rangelands of the western United States. This is one part of a broader ongoing collaboration between the Washington State University's (WSU) Center for Sustaining Agriculture and Natural Resources (CSANR) to characterize cattle production risks and management implications of a changing climate in the United States. Our goal is to give users a way to identify the regions that are most at-risk for reduced capacity. Users should also be able to quantify potential for regional redistributions in rangeland cattle inventory, and characterize their macroscale implications. This is to inform adaptation planning by the industry and affected communities, with a view to maximize benefits and minimize losses. Rangelands are grasslands, woodlands, wetlands, and deserts that are grazed by domestic livestock or wild animals. They contain all native vegetation that is not human influenced. The rangelands regions that we are concerned with are: Northwest, Southwest, Northern Plains, Southern Plains
